ABOUT ME

A technical blog documenting my journey in Mechatronics and Cybersecurity. Focused on Hardware Hacking, Industrial Protocols (OT), and IT security research.

Hi! I’m Pablo Paucar, and welcome to my technical space.

This blog is my personal repository for everything at the intersection of Mechatronics and Digital Security. It’s a place where I share my research, projects, and ideas on how the gap between mechanical design and information technology can be bridged and, more importantly, secured.

The goal of the blog is to document technical insights and concepts that I believe are worth sharing. Whether it’s a deep dive into Industrial Cybersecurity (OT/ICS), a case study on hardware exploitation, or the implementation of security frameworks like ISA/IEC 62443 in critical infrastructure, this space is dedicated to the resilience of modern engineering.

What I Do

I am a Mechatronic Engineer with a passion for securing the physical layer of technology. My journey is rooted in a deep interest in hardware hacking and the complex relationship between industrial control systems and digital networks.

My work focuses on analyzing physical devices and electronic components to identify vulnerabilities, ensuring that as systems become more connected, they also become more robust. I strive to translate the complexity of industrial protocols into secure, reliable engineering practices.
